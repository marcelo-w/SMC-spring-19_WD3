Monte Albán

A large pre-Columbian archaeological site in the southern Mexican state of Oaxaca. Founded 500 BC and occupied until AD 500-750.

Location

The site is located on a low mountainous range rising above the plain in the central section of the Valley of Oaxaca. The present-day state capital Oaxaca City is located approximately 9 km (6 mi) east of Monte Albán.

Pyramids

Mesoamerican pyramids sites, unlike those found in Egypt, didn’t function as tombs. The stepped structures formed a prominent part of ancient Mesoamerican architecture.

Monuments

  • Main Plaza

  • Tombs

Social Stratification

Walls that were as large as nine meters tall and twenty meters wide were built around the settlement and would have been used not only to create a boundary between Monte Albán and neighboring settlements but also prove the power of the elites within the community.

Carved Stones

Numerous stone carvings represent tortured, sacrificed war prisoners, some identified by name, and may depict leaders of competing centers and villages captured by Monte Albán.

Ballcourts

Two ballcourts are known to have existed at the site. The ballgame was a sport with ritual associations played since 1400 BC. The sport had different versions in different places during the millennia, and a newer more modern version of the game, ulama, is still played in a few places by the indigenous population.
